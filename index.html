Yarn Weights

1) Lace weight (also known as super fine weight)is finest and thinnest of yarns. Recommended needle size is US 000-1. It is often knit with a larger needle to create lacy, openwork patterns. Mohair and crochet thread are examples of lace weight. Click here to see examples of projects.

2) Fingering weight (also known as fine weight) is super fine and is often referred to as sock yarn. Recommend needle size is US 1-3. It is great for socks, shawls, baby garments, and accessories. Click here to see examples of projects.

3) Sport weight (also known as light weight) is thicker than fingering weight and often referred to as baby weight. Recommended needle size US 3-5. It is often used for colorwork and cabling projects. Click here to see examples of projects.

4) Medium weight is most commonly referred to as worsted weight. Recommended needle size US 7-9. It is the most versatile. It can be used to make garments, blankets, home decor, and anything else. Click here to see examples of projects.

5) Bulky weight is about two times thicker than worsted weight. Recommended needle size US 9-11. It is great for knitting something quickly. Great projects would be thick sweater, blankets, and rugs. Click here to see examples of projects.

6) Super bulky is very thick. Recommended needle size US 11-17. It is great for cowls, scarves, and hats. Click here to see examples of projects.

7) Jumbo weight is the thickest of yarns. Recommended needle size US 17 or larger. It is great for knitting or "big stitch" projects. Click here to see examples of projects.
